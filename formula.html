Chapter 1

Introduction to Statics

1/3 Vectors & Components

Vector Sum (Parallelogram)
$$\mathbf{V} = \mathbf{V}_1 + \mathbf{V}_2$$
Magnitude (2D)
$$V = \sqrt{V_x^2 + V_y^2}$$
Direction Angle
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{V_y}{V_x}\right)$$
Unit Vector Form
$$\mathbf{V} = V\,\hat{\mathbf{n}}$$
$\hat{\mathbf{n}}$ has magnitude 1 in the direction of $\mathbf{V}$
3D Component Form
$$\mathbf{V} = V_x\,\mathbf{i} + V_y\,\mathbf{j} + V_z\,\mathbf{k}$$
Magnitude (3D)
$$V = \sqrt{V_x^2 + V_y^2 + V_z^2}$$
Direction Cosines
$$l = \cos\theta_x,\quad m = \cos\theta_y,\quad n = \cos\theta_z$$
$$l^2 + m^2 + n^2 = 1$$
Components via Direction Cosines
$$V_x = lV,\quad V_y = mV,\quad V_z = nV$$

1/4 Newton's Laws

Newton's 2nd Law Eq 1/1
$$\mathbf{F} = m\mathbf{a}$$
Law of Gravitation Eq 1/2
$$F = G\,\frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
$G = 6.673\times10^{-11}\ \text{m}^3/(\text{kg·s}^2)$
Weight
$$W = mg$$

1/5 Units & Gravitational Acceleration

Newton (SI) Definition
$$1\,\text{N} = 1\,\text{kg·m/s}^2$$
Slug (US) Definition
$$1\,\text{slug} = \frac{1\,\text{lb·sec}^2}{\text{ft}}$$
Gravitational Acceleration
$$g = 9.81\ \text{m/s}^2 \quad \text{(SI)}$$
$$g = 32.2\ \text{ft/sec}^2 \quad \text{(US)}$$
Small Angle Approximations (rad)
$$\sin\theta \approx \theta,\quad \tan\theta \approx \theta,\quad \cos\theta \approx 1$$

Chapter 2

Force Systems

2/3 Rectangular Components (2D)

Force Decomposition Eq 2/1–2
$$\mathbf{F} = F_x\,\mathbf{i} + F_y\,\mathbf{j}$$
Scalar Components Eq 2/3
$$F_x = F\cos\theta, \qquad F_y = F\sin\theta$$
Magnitude & Direction
$$F = \sqrt{F_x^2 + F_y^2}, \qquad \theta = \tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{F_y}{F_x}\right)$$
Resultant Components Eq 2/4
$$R_x = \textstyle\sum F_x, \qquad R_y = \textstyle\sum F_y$$
Resultant Magnitude & Angle
$$R = \sqrt{(\textstyle\sum F_x)^2 + (\textstyle\sum F_y)^2}$$
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{\textstyle\sum F_y}{\textstyle\sum F_x}\right)$$

2/4 Moment of a Force

Scalar Moment Eq 2/5
$$M = F\,d$$
$d$ = perpendicular (moment arm) distance from axis to line of action
Cross-Product Moment Eq 2/6
$$\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{r} \times \mathbf{F}$$
Magnitude via Cross Product Eq 2/7
$$M = Fr\sin\alpha = Fd$$
$\alpha$ = angle between $\mathbf{r}$ and $\mathbf{F}$
Cross-Product Expansion (3D)
$$\mathbf{M}_O = \begin{vmatrix}\mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k}\\ r_x & r_y & r_z\\ F_x & F_y & F_z\end{vmatrix} = (r_y F_z - r_z F_y)\,\mathbf{i} + (r_z F_x - r_x F_z)\,\mathbf{j} + (r_x F_y - r_y F_x)\,\mathbf{k}$$
Varignon's Theorem Eq 2/8
$$\mathbf{M}_O = \mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{R} = \mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{P} + \mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{Q}$$
Moment of resultant = sum of moments of components
Moment about an Axis
$$M_\lambda = \hat{\mathbf{n}}_\lambda \cdot (\mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{F})$$
Scalar triple product; $\hat{\mathbf{n}}_\lambda$ = unit vector along axis $\lambda$

2/5 Couple

Couple Magnitude
$$M = Fd$$
Two equal, opposite, non-collinear forces $F$ separated by distance $d$. Independent of moment center.
Couple — Vector Form
$$\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{F}$$
$\mathbf{r}$ runs from any point on $-\mathbf{F}$ to any point on $\mathbf{F}$. Couple is a free vector.
Units of Moment / Couple
$$\text{SI: } \text{N·m} \qquad \text{US: } \text{lb·ft}$$

2/6 Resultants of Force Systems

Coplanar Resultant Eq 2/9
$$\mathbf{R} = \textstyle\sum\mathbf{F}, \qquad R_x = \textstyle\sum F_x, \quad R_y = \textstyle\sum F_y$$ $$R = \sqrt{(\textstyle\sum F_x)^2 + (\textstyle\sum F_y)^2}, \qquad \theta = \tan^{-1}\!\left(\frac{\textstyle\sum F_y}{\textstyle\sum F_x}\right)$$
Principle of Moments Eq 2/10
$$\mathbf{R} = \textstyle\sum\mathbf{F}, \qquad M_O = \textstyle\sum M = \textstyle\sum(Fd)$$ $$Rd = M_O \quad\Longrightarrow\quad d = \frac{M_O}{R}$$
$d$ = perpendicular distance from reference point $O$ to line of action of $\mathbf{R}$

2/7–2/9 Three-Dimensional Force Systems

3D Force in Components
$$\mathbf{F} = F_x\,\mathbf{i} + F_y\,\mathbf{j} + F_z\,\mathbf{k}$$
Unit Vector from Two Points
$$\hat{\mathbf{n}} = \frac{(x_B - x_A)\mathbf{i} + (y_B - y_A)\mathbf{j} + (z_B - z_A)\mathbf{k}}{|AB|}$$
3D Resultant
$$\mathbf{R} = \textstyle\sum\mathbf{F}, \qquad \mathbf{M}_O = \textstyle\sum(\mathbf{r}\times\mathbf{F})$$
Chapter 3

Equilibrium

3/3 Equilibrium Conditions (2D)

General Equilibrium Eq 3/1
$$\mathbf{R} = \textstyle\sum\mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}, \qquad \mathbf{M} = \textstyle\sum\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{0}$$
Scalar Equations (2D) Eq 3/2
$$\sum F_x = 0, \qquad \sum F_y = 0, \qquad \sum M_O = 0$$
Alternative Set 1
$$\sum F_x = 0, \qquad \sum M_A = 0, \qquad \sum M_B = 0$$
$A$ and $B$ must not lie on a line ⊥ to $x$-direction
Alternative Set 2 (3 Moments)
$$\sum M_A = 0, \qquad \sum M_B = 0, \qquad \sum M_C = 0$$
$A$, $B$, $C$ not collinear
Two-Force Member
$$\mathbf{F}_1 = -\mathbf{F}_2 \quad\text{(equal, opposite, collinear)}$$
Three-Force Member
$$\text{Lines of action must be concurrent}$$

3/4 Equilibrium Conditions (3D)

Six Scalar Equations
$$\sum F_x = 0, \quad \sum F_y = 0, \quad \sum F_z = 0$$ $$\sum M_x = 0, \quad \sum M_y = 0, \quad \sum M_z = 0$$

3/2 Spring Forces

Linear Spring
$$F = kx$$
$k$ = stiffness (N/m or lb/ft); $x$ = deformation from neutral position
Torsional Spring
$$M = k_T\,\theta$$
$k_T$ = torsional stiffness (N·m/rad); $\theta$ = angular deflection (rad)
Chapter 4

Structures

4/2 Plane & Space Truss Criteria

Plane Truss — Statically Determinate
$$m + 3 = 2j$$
$m$ = members, $j$ = joints.   If $m+3 > 2j$: indeterminate; $m+3 < 2j$: unstable
Space Truss — Statically Determinate
$$m + 6 = 3j$$

4/3 Method of Joints

Equilibrium at Each Pin (2D)
$$\sum F_x = 0, \qquad \sum F_y = 0$$
Arrow away from pin → tension (T); arrow toward pin → compression (C)
Equilibrium at Each Joint (3D)
$$\sum F_x = 0, \quad \sum F_y = 0, \quad \sum F_z = 0$$
Zero-Force Member Conditions
$$\text{Case 1: Two non-collinear members at unloaded joint} \Rightarrow F_1 = F_2 = 0$$ $$\text{Case 2: Two collinear + one non-collinear (unloaded)} \Rightarrow F_3 = 0,\ F_1 = F_2$$ $$\text{Case 3: Two pairs of collinear members} \Rightarrow \text{forces in each pair equal & opposite}$$

4/4 Method of Sections

Three Equilibrium Equations per Cut
$$\sum F_x = 0, \qquad \sum F_y = 0, \qquad \sum M_O = 0$$
Cut no more than 3 unknown members. Choose moment center $O$ at intersection of two unknowns.
Chapter 5

Distributed Forces

5/2–5/3 Center of Mass & Centroids

Center of Mass Coordinates Eq 5/1b
$$\bar{x} = \frac{\int x\,dm}{m}, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{\int y\,dm}{m}, \quad \bar{z} = \frac{\int z\,dm}{m}$$
Center of Mass — Vector Form Eq 5/2
$$\bar{\mathbf{r}} = \frac{\int \mathbf{r}\,dm}{m}$$
Variable Density Eq 5/3
$$\bar{x} = \frac{\int x\rho\,dV}{\int\rho\,dV}, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{\int y\rho\,dV}{\int\rho\,dV}, \quad \bar{z} = \frac{\int z\rho\,dV}{\int\rho\,dV}$$
Centroid of a Line Eq 5/4
$$\bar{x} = \frac{\int x\,dL}{L}, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{\int y\,dL}{L}, \quad \bar{z} = \frac{\int z\,dL}{L}$$
Centroid of an Area Eq 5/5
$$\bar{x} = \frac{\int x\,dA}{A}, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{\int y\,dA}{A}, \quad \bar{z} = \frac{\int z\,dA}{A}$$
Centroid of a Volume Eq 5/6
$$\bar{x} = \frac{\int x\,dV}{V}, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{\int y\,dV}{V}, \quad \bar{z} = \frac{\int z\,dV}{V}$$
Using Centroid of Element Eq 5/5a
$$\bar{x} = \frac{\int x_c\,dA}{A}, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{\int y_c\,dA}{A}$$
Use the centroidal coordinate $x_c, y_c$ of the differential element, not its boundary coordinate

5/4 Composite Bodies

Composite Mass Center Eq 5/7
$$\bar{X} = \frac{\sum m\bar{x}}{\sum m}, \qquad \bar{Y} = \frac{\sum m\bar{y}}{\sum m}, \qquad \bar{Z} = \frac{\sum m\bar{z}}{\sum m}$$
Replace $m$ with $L$, $A$, or $V$ for lines, areas, or volumes. Holes/cavities are negative.
Approximation (irregular areas)
$$\bar{x} = \frac{\sum A\,x_c}{\sum A}, \qquad \bar{y} = \frac{\sum A\,y_c}{\sum A}$$

5/6–5/7 Beams — Distributed Loads & Internal Effects

Resultant of Distributed Load
$$R = \int w\,dx, \qquad \bar{x} = \frac{\int x\,w\,dx}{R}$$
$w$ = load intensity (force/length); resultant acts at centroid of load diagram
Uniform Load
$$R = wL \quad\text{at } x = \tfrac{L}{2}$$
Triangular Load
$$R = \tfrac{1}{2}w_{\max}L \quad\text{at } x = \tfrac{L}{3}\ (\text{from large end})$$
Load–Shear Relationship Eq 5/10
$$w = -\frac{dV}{dx}$$
Slope of shear diagram = $-w$
Integrated Load–Shear
$$V = V_0 - \int_{x_0}^{x} w\,dx$$
Change in shear = $-$(area under load curve)
Shear–Moment Relationship Eq 5/11
$$V = \frac{dM}{dx}$$
Slope of moment diagram = $V$
Integrated Shear–Moment
$$M = M_0 + \int_{x_0}^{x} V\,dx$$
Change in moment = area under shear diagram
Load–Moment Relationship Eq 5/12
$$\frac{d^2M}{dx^2} = -w$$
$M$ is two degrees higher in $x$ than $w$; max/min $M$ occurs where $V = 0$
Chapter 6

Friction

6/3 Dry (Coulomb) Friction

Maximum Static Friction Eq 6/1
$$F_{\max} = \mu_s N$$
⚠ Applies only at impending motion. Otherwise $F < \mu_s N$.
Kinetic Friction Eq 6/2
$$F_k = \mu_k N$$
$\mu_k < \mu_s$ generally
Friction Angles
$$\tan\phi_s = \mu_s, \qquad \tan\phi_k = \mu_k$$
$\phi_s$ = angle of static friction; $\phi_k$ = angle of kinetic friction

6/4–6/5 Wedges & Screws

Wedge Self-Locking Condition
$$\alpha \leq \phi \quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad \alpha \leq \tan^{-1}\mu$$
$\alpha$ = wedge angle; $\phi$ = friction angle
Screw — Lead Angle
$$\tan\lambda = \frac{l}{2\pi r}$$
$l$ = lead (advance per revolution); $r$ = mean thread radius
Screw Self-Locking Condition
$$\lambda \leq \phi$$
Screw Efficiency
$$e = \frac{Wl}{2\pi M} = \frac{\tan\lambda}{\tan(\lambda+\phi)}$$
Appendix A

Area Moments of Inertia

A/2 Definitions & Radius of Gyration

Rectangular Moments of Inertia Eq A/1
$$I_x = \int y^2\,dA, \qquad I_y = \int x^2\,dA$$
Polar Moment of Inertia Eq A/2
$$I_z = J = \int r^2\,dA$$
Polar–Rectangular Relation Eq A/3
$$I_z = I_x + I_y$$
Radii of Gyration Eq A/4
$$k_x = \sqrt{\frac{I_x}{A}}, \quad k_y = \sqrt{\frac{I_y}{A}}, \quad k_z = \sqrt{\frac{I_z}{A}}$$
Polar Radius of Gyration Eq A/5
$$k_z^2 = k_x^2 + k_y^2$$

A/2 Parallel-Axis Theorem

Transfer of Rectangular MOI Eq A/6
$$I_x = \bar{I}_x + A\,d_x^2, \qquad I_y = \bar{I}_y + A\,d_y^2$$
$\bar{I}$ = centroidal MOI; $d$ = distance from centroid to new axis. One axis must pass through centroid.
Transfer of Polar MOI Eq A/6a
$$I_z = \bar{I}_z + A\,d^2$$
Transfer of Radius of Gyration Eq A/6b
$$k^2 = \bar{k}^2 + d^2$$
Product of Inertia
$$I_{xy} = \int xy\,dA$$
Zero if either axis is an axis of symmetry
Transfer of Product of Inertia
$$I_{xy} = \bar{I}_{xy} + A\,d_x\,d_y$$
Rotation of Axes
$$I_{x'} = \frac{I_x + I_y}{2} + \frac{I_x - I_y}{2}\cos 2\theta - I_{xy}\sin 2\theta$$ $$I_{y'} = \frac{I_x + I_y}{2} - \frac{I_x - I_y}{2}\cos 2\theta + I_{xy}\sin 2\theta$$ $$I_{x'y'} = \frac{I_x - I_y}{2}\sin 2\theta + I_{xy}\cos 2\theta$$
Principal Axes Angle
$$\tan 2\theta_0 = \frac{-2I_{xy}}{I_x - I_y}$$
Principal Moments of Inertia
$$I_{\max,\min} = \frac{I_x + I_y}{2} \pm \sqrt{\left(\frac{I_x - I_y}{2}\right)^2 + I_{xy}^2}$$
