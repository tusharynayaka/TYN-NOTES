Integral Calculus Formula Handbook

Engineering Mathematics – Unit I

Double Integrals

$$\iint_R f(x,y)\,dA$$
$$\iint_R f(x,y)\,dx\,dy$$
$$\iint_R f(x,y)\,dy\,dx$$

Jacobian

$$ J=\frac{\partial(u,v)}{\partial(x,y)} =\begin{vmatrix} \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial u}{\partial y}\\ \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \end{vmatrix} $$
$$ J=\frac{\partial(u,v,w)}{\partial(x,y,z)} $$

Change of Variables

$$ \iint_R f(x,y)\,dxdy =\iint_{\bar R}\phi(u,v)\left|J\right|\,dudv $$
$$ \iiint_V f(x,y,z)\,dV =\iiint_{\bar V}\phi(u,v,w)\left|J\right|\,dudvdw $$

Polar Coordinates

$$x=r\cos\theta,\qquad y=r\sin\theta$$
$$dx\,dy=r\,dr\,d\theta$$
$$ \iint_R f(x,y)\,dxdy =\iint_{\bar R} f(r\cos\theta,r\sin\theta)\,r\,dr\,d\theta $$

Area & Volume

$$A=\iint_R dA$$
$$V=\iint_R z\,dA$$
$$ f_{avg}=\frac{\iint_R f(x,y)\,dA}{\iint_R dA} $$

Triple Integrals

$$\iiint_V f(x,y,z)\,dV$$
$$M=\iiint_V \rho\,dV$$

Cylindrical Coordinates

$$ x=\rho\cos\phi,\quad y=\rho\sin\phi,\quad z=z $$
$$ dV=\rho\,d\rho\,d\phi\,dz $$

Spherical Coordinates

$$ x=r\sin\theta\cos\phi $$ $$ y=r\sin\theta\sin\phi $$ $$ z=r\cos\theta $$
$$ dV=r^2\sin\theta\,dr\,d\theta\,d\phi $$

Moment of Inertia (Plane Lamina)

$$I_x=\iint_D y^2\rho\,dA$$
$$I_y=\iint_D x^2\rho\,dA$$
$$I_O=I_x+I_y$$

Moment of Inertia (Solid)

$$I_x=\iiint_V (y^2+z^2)\rho\,dV$$
$$I_y=\iiint_V (x^2+z^2)\rho\,dV$$
$$I_z=\iiint_V (x^2+y^2)\rho\,dV$$

Centre of Gravity / Centre of Mass

$$ \bar x=\frac{\iint_D x\rho\,dA}{M}, \qquad \bar y=\frac{\iint_D y\rho\,dA}{M} $$
$$ \bar x=\frac{\iiint_V x\rho\,dV}{M}, \quad \bar y=\frac{\iiint_V y\rho\,dV}{M}, \quad \bar z=\frac{\iiint_V z\rho\,dV}{M} $$
