∬Double Integral
Definition & Fubini's Theorem

The double integral adds up \(f(x,y)\) over every tiny area tile \(dA\) inside region \(R\).

Rectangular\(\displaystyle\iint_R f\,dA = \int_a^b\!\int_c^d f(x,y)\,dy\,dx = \int_c^d\!\int_a^b f(x,y)\,dx\,dy\)
Type I\(\displaystyle\iint_R f\,dA = \int_a^b\!\int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} f(x,y)\,dy\,dx\)
Type II\(\displaystyle\iint_R f\,dA = \int_c^d\!\int_{h_1(y)}^{h_2(y)} f(x,y)\,dx\,dy\)
Properties
Linearity\(\displaystyle\iint_R [f+g]\,dA = \iint_R f\,dA + \iint_R g\,dA\)
Scalar\(\displaystyle\iint_R c\,f\,dA = c\iint_R f\,dA\)
Monotone\(f\le g\;\Rightarrow\;\iint_R f\,dA \le \iint_R g\,dA\)
Applications
QuantityFormula
Area of \(R\)\(\displaystyle A = \iint_R 1\,dA\)
Volume below \(z=f(x,y)\)\(\displaystyle V = \iint_R f(x,y)\,dA\)
Average value\(\displaystyle f_\text{avg} = \dfrac{1}{A(R)}\iint_R f\,dA\)
Surface area of \(z=f(x,y)\)\(\displaystyle SA = \iint_R \sqrt{1+f_x^2+f_y^2}\,dA\)
Mass (density \(\delta\))\(\displaystyle M = \iint_D \delta(x,y)\,dA\)
Centre of mass \(\bar{x}\)\(\displaystyle \bar{x} = \frac{1}{M}\iint_D x\,\delta\,dA\)
Centre of mass \(\bar{y}\)\(\displaystyle \bar{y} = \frac{1}{M}\iint_D y\,\delta\,dA\)
Area (polar)\(\displaystyle A = \iint r\,dr\,d\theta\)
Jacobian & Change of Variables (2D)

Transformation \(x=g(u,v),\; y=h(u,v)\). The Jacobian measures how area scales under this mapping.

Jacobian\(\displaystyle J(u,v) = \frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)} = \begin{vmatrix}\dfrac{\partial x}{\partial u}&\dfrac{\partial x}{\partial v}\\[8pt]\dfrac{\partial y}{\partial u}&\dfrac{\partial y}{\partial v}\end{vmatrix} = \frac{\partial x}{\partial u}\frac{\partial y}{\partial v}-\frac{\partial x}{\partial v}\frac{\partial y}{\partial u}\)
Change rule\(\displaystyle\iint_R f(x,y)\,dA = \iint_S f\bigl(g(u,v),h(u,v)\bigr)\,|J|\,du\,dv\)
Polar\(x=r\cos\theta,\quad y=r\sin\theta,\quad J=r\)
Polar form\(\displaystyle\iint f(x,y)\,dx\,dy = \iint f(r\cos\theta,\,r\sin\theta)\;r\,dr\,d\theta\)
Property\(J_{(x,y)\to(u,v)}\;\times\; J_{(u,v)\to(x,y)} = 1\)
When to use polar: When the integrand or region involves \(x^2+y^2\), or the region is a circle or sector.
Change of Order of Integration

Steps: (1) Sketch region \(R\) using original limits. (2) Re-express limits for the swapped order. (3) Rewrite & integrate.

Example\(\displaystyle\int_0^a\!\int_x^a f(x,y)\,dy\,dx \;=\; \int_0^a\!\int_0^y f(x,y)\,dx\,dy\)
Tip: Changing order can turn an impossible inner integral into an easy one!

🎲Probability Basics
Key Definitions
  • Sample Space \(S\): Set of all possible outcomes of an experiment
  • Event \(E\): Any subset of \(S\)
  • Mutually exclusive: \(A\cap B=\emptyset\) — they cannot occur together
  • Exhaustive: \(A\cup B=S\) — together they cover every possibility
  • Equally likely: Every outcome has the same probability
Axioms & Key Rules
Classical\(\displaystyle P(E)=\frac{n(E)}{n(S)}\)
Axiom 1\(0\le P(E)\le 1\)
Axiom 2\(P(S)=1\)
Complement\(P(\bar{E})=1-P(E)\)
Addition\(P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)\)
Mut. excl.\(A\cap B=\emptyset\;\Rightarrow\; P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)\)
XRandom Variables
Discrete RV — PMF, CDF, Mean, Variance
PMF\(p(x)=P(X=x),\quad\displaystyle\sum_x p(x)=1\)
CDF\(\displaystyle F(x)=P(X\le x)=\sum_{x_i\le x}p(x_i)\)
Mean\(\displaystyle\mu=E(X)=\sum_x x\,P(X=x)\)
Variance\(\displaystyle\sigma^2=E(X^2)-[E(X)]^2=\sum_x x^2 P(X=x)-\mu^2\)
Std Dev\(\sigma=\sqrt{\sigma^2}\)
Linear Functions — \(Y=aX+b\)
Mean\(E(aX+b)=a\,E(X)+b\)
Variance\(\operatorname{Var}(aX+b)=a^2\operatorname{Var}(X)\)
Std Dev\(\sigma_{aX+b}=|a|\,\sigma_X\)
Continuous RV — PDF, CDF, Mean, Variance
PDF\(f(x)\ge0,\quad\displaystyle\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}f(x)\,dx=1\)
CDF\(\displaystyle F(x)=\int_{-\infty}^x f(t)\,dt,\quad P(a\le X\le b)=\int_a^b f(x)\,dx\)
Mean\(\displaystyle E(X)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x\,f(x)\,dx\)
Variance\(\displaystyle\operatorname{Var}(X)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x^2 f(x)\,dx-[E(X)]^2\)
📊Probability Distributions
Bernoulli Distribution — single trial
PMF\(P(X=1)=p,\quad P(X=0)=q=1-p\)
Mean\(E(X)=p\)
Variance\(\operatorname{Var}(X)=pq=p(1-p)\)
Binomial Distribution — \(B(n,p)\)

Conditions: Fixed \(n\) independent trials, constant probability \(p\), two outcomes per trial.

PMF\(\displaystyle P(X=r)=\binom{n}{r}p^r q^{n-r},\quad r=0,1,\ldots,n,\quad q=1-p\)
Mean\(\mu=np\)
Variance\(\sigma^2=npq\)
Std Dev\(\sigma=\sqrt{npq}\)
Normalisation\(\displaystyle\sum_{r=0}^n\binom{n}{r}p^r q^{n-r}=(p+q)^n=1\)
Poisson Distribution — \(P(\lambda)\)

Conditions: \(n\to\infty,\;p\to0,\;\lambda=np\) finite. Mean = Variance (unique property!).

PMF\(\displaystyle P(X=x)=\frac{\lambda^x e^{-\lambda}}{x!},\quad x=0,1,2,\ldots\)
Mean\(E(X)=\lambda\)
Variance\(\operatorname{Var}(X)=\lambda\)
Exponential Distribution — \(\operatorname{Exp}(\lambda)\)

Models waiting time; has the memoryless property.

CDF\(F(x)=1-e^{-\lambda x},\quad x>0\)
Mean\(\mu=\dfrac{1}{\lambda}\)
Variance\(\sigma^2=\dfrac{1}{\lambda^2}\)

Normal Distribution — \(N(\mu,\sigma^2)\)

The famous bell curve, symmetric about mean \(\mu\).

PDF\(\displaystyle f(x)=\frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}\exp\!\left[-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right]\)
Standardise\(\displaystyle Z=\frac{X-\mu}{\sigma}\;\sim\;N(0,1)\)
Std PDF\(\displaystyle\varphi(z)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-z^2/2}\)
68-95-99 rule\(P(\mu-\sigma\le X\le\mu+\sigma)=68.27\%\)
\(P(\mu-2\sigma\le X\le\mu+2\sigma)=94.45\%\)
\(P(\mu-3\sigma\le X\le\mu+3\sigma)=99.73\%\)
Probability\(\displaystyle P(a\le X\le b)=\Phi\!\left(\frac{b-\mu}{\sigma}\right)-\Phi\!\left(\frac{a-\mu}{\sigma}\right)\)

Working Procedure
  1. Take \(\mathcal{L}\) of both sides of the ODE, substituting \(y(0),y'(0),\ldots\)
  2. Solve algebraically for \(Y(s)=\mathcal{L}\{y(t)\}\)
  3. Take \(\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{Y(s)\}\) to obtain \(y(t)\)

∿Fourier Series — Full Range
Dirichlet's Conditions
  • \(f(x)\) is periodic and single-valued
  • \(f(x)\) is continuous, or has only a finite number of finite discontinuities per period
  • \(f(x)\) has a finite number of maxima and minima per period
