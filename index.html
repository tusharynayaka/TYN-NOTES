∬Double Integral
Definition & Fubini's Theorem
Rectangular\(\displaystyle\iint_R f\,dA = \int_a^b\!\int_c^d f(x,y)\,dy\,dx = \int_c^d\!\int_a^b f(x,y)\,dx\,dy\)
Type I\(\displaystyle\iint_R f\,dA = \int_a^b\!\int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} f(x,y)\,dy\,dx\)
Type II\(\displaystyle\iint_R f\,dA = \int_c^d\!\int_{h_1(y)}^{h_2(y)} f(x,y)\,dx\,dy\)
Properties
Linearity\(\displaystyle\iint_R [f+g]\,dA = \iint_R f\,dA + \iint_R g\,dA\)
Scalar\(\displaystyle\iint_R c\,f\,dA = c\iint_R f\,dA\)
Monotone\(f\le g\;\Rightarrow\;\iint_R f\,dA \le \iint_R g\,dA\)
Applications
QuantityFormula
Area of \(R\)\(\displaystyle A = \iint_R 1\,dA\)
Volume below \(z=f(x,y)\)\(\displaystyle V = \iint_R f(x,y)\,dA\)
Average value\(\displaystyle f_\text{avg} = \dfrac{1}{A(R)}\iint_R f\,dA\)
Mass (density \(\delta\))\(\displaystyle M = \iint_D \delta(x,y)\,dA\)
Centre of mass \(\bar{x},\bar{y}\)\(\displaystyle \bar{x}=\frac{1}{M}\iint_D x\delta\,dA,\quad \bar{y}=\frac{1}{M}\iint_D y\delta\,dA\)
Jacobian & Change of Variables
Jacobian\(\displaystyle J(u,v) = \frac{\partial(x,y)}{\partial(u,v)} = \begin{vmatrix}x_u&x_v\\y_u&y_v\end{vmatrix}\)
Change rule\(\displaystyle\iint_R f(x,y)\,dA = \iint_S f(g(u,v),h(u,v))\,|J|\,du\,dv\)
Polar\(x=r\cos\theta,\;y=r\sin\theta,\;J=r\)
Polar integral\(\displaystyle\iint f(x,y)\,dx\,dy = \iint f(r\cos\theta,r\sin\theta)\,r\,dr\,d\theta\)
💡 Use polar when: integrand or region involves \(x^2+y^2\) or circular boundaries.
Change of Order of Integration
Example\(\displaystyle\int_0^a\!\int_x^a f(x,y)\,dy\,dx \;=\; \int_0^a\!\int_0^y f(x,y)\,dx\,dy\)
Tip: Sketch region first; swap limits to simplify integration.
∭Triple Integral
Definition & Average Value
Triple integral\(\displaystyle\iiint_E f(x,y,z)\,dV\)
Average value\(\displaystyle f_\text{avg} = \frac{1}{V(E)}\iiint_E f\,dV,\quad V(E)=\iiint_E 1\,dV\)
Applications
QuantityFormulaVolume\(\displaystyle V=\iiint_E 1\,dV\)Mass (\(\rho\) density)\(\displaystyle M=\iiint_Q \rho\,dV\)Moments: \(M_{yz},M_{xz},M_{xy}\)\(\iiint x\rho\,dV,\;\iiint y\rho\,dV,\;\iiint z\rho\,dV\)Centre of mass\(\bar{x}=M_{yz}/M,\;\bar{y}=M_{xz}/M,\;\bar{z}=M_{xy}/M\)
Cylindrical Coordinates
Transform\(x=r\cos\theta,\;y=r\sin\theta,\;z=z\)
Volume element\(dV=r\,dr\,d\theta\,dz\)
Integral\(\displaystyle\iiint f\,dV = \iiint f(r\cos\theta,r\sin\theta,z)\,r\,dr\,d\theta\,dz\)
Use for regions symmetric about \(z\)-axis (cylinders, cones).
Spherical Coordinates
Transform\(x=\rho\sin\phi\cos\theta,\;y=\rho\sin\phi\sin\theta,\;z=\rho\cos\phi\)
Volume element\(dV=\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta\)
Sphere volume\(\displaystyle V=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\int_0^{\pi}\!\int_0^a\rho^2\sin\phi\,d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta=\frac{4}{3}\pi a^3\)
Use for spherical symmetry (balls, cones, \(\rho^2\) integrands).
Moment of Inertia (2D)
\(I_x\)\(\displaystyle \iint_D y^2\rho\,dA\)
\(I_y\)\(\displaystyle \iint_D x^2\rho\,dA\)
\(I_0\) (polar)\(\displaystyle I_x+I_y\)
Moment of Inertia (3D)
About \(x\)\(\displaystyle \iiint (y^2+z^2)\rho\,dV\)
About \(y\)\(\displaystyle \iiint (x^2+z^2)\rho\,dV\)
About \(z\)\(\displaystyle \iiint (x^2+y^2)\rho\,dV\)
Unit 2 — Probability & Random Variables
Probability axioms · random variables · PMF, PDF, CDF · mean & variance · Bernoulli, Binomial, Poisson, Exponential, Normal distributions
🎲Probability Basics
Key Definitions
  • Sample space \(S\): all outcomes
  • Event \(E\subseteq S\)
  • Mutually exclusive: \(A\cap B=\emptyset\)
  • Exhaustive: \(A\cup B=S\)
  • Equally likely: each outcome same chance
Axioms & Rules
Classical\(P(E)=\frac{n(E)}{n(S)}\)
Axiom 1-2\(0\le P(E)\le1,\;P(S)=1\)
Complement\(P(\bar{E})=1-P(E)\)
Addition\(P(A\cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A\cap B)\)
XRandom Variables
Discrete RV — PMF, CDF, Mean, Variance
PMF\(p(x)=P(X=x),\;\sum p(x)=1\)
CDF\(F(x)=P(X\le x)\)
Mean\(\displaystyle \mu=E(X)=\sum x\,p(x)\)
Variance\(\displaystyle \sigma^2=E(X^2)-\mu^2\)
Linear Functions \(Y=aX+b\)
Mean\(E(aX+b)=aE(X)+b\)
Variance\(\operatorname{Var}(aX+b)=a^2\operatorname{Var}(X)\)
Continuous RV — PDF, CDF, Mean, Variance
PDF\(f(x)\ge0,\;\int_{-\infty}^\infty f(x)dx=1\)
CDF\(F(x)=\int_{-\infty}^x f(t)dt\)
Mean\(\displaystyle E(X)=\int_{-\infty}^\infty x f(x)dx\)
Variance\(\displaystyle \int x^2 f(x)dx -[E(X)]^2\)
📊Probability Distributions
Bernoulli\((p)\)
PMF\(P(X=1)=p,\;P(X=0)=1-p\)
Mean\(p\)
Variance\(p(1-p)\)
Binomial\((n,p)\)
PMF\(\displaystyle P(X=r)=\binom{n}{r}p^r q^{n-r},\;q=1-p\)
Mean\(np\)
Variance\(npq\)
Poisson\((\lambda)\)
PMF\(\displaystyle P(X=x)=\frac{\lambda^x e^{-\lambda}}{x!},\;x=0,1,2,\dots\)
Mean\(\lambda\)
Variance\(\lambda\)
Exponential\((\lambda)\)
PDF\(f(x)=\lambda e^{-\lambda x},\;x>0\)
CDF\(F(x)=1-e^{-\lambda x}\)
Mean\(1/\lambda\)
Variance\(1/\lambda^2\)
Normal\((\mu,\sigma^2)\)
PDF\(\displaystyle f(x)=\frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}}e^{-(x-\mu)^2/(2\sigma^2)}\)
Standardise\(\displaystyle Z=\frac{X-\mu}{\sigma}\sim N(0,1)\)
68-95-99.7 rule\(\mu\pm\sigma\) (68%), \(\mu\pm2\sigma\) (95%), \(\mu\pm3\sigma\) (99.7%)
Probability\(P(a\le X\le b)=\Phi(\frac{b-\mu}{\sigma})-\Phi(\frac{a-\mu}{\sigma})\)

ℒDefinition & Table
Definition
Laplace transform\(\displaystyle F(s)=\int_0^{\infty}e^{-st}f(t)dt\)
Standard Laplace Table
\(f(t)\)\(F(s)\)\(1\)\(\frac{1}{s}\)\(t^n\) (integer)\(\frac{n!}{s^{n+1}}\)\(e^{at}\)\(\frac{1}{s-a}\)\(\sin at\)\(\frac{a}{s^2+a^2}\)\(\cos at\)\(\frac{s}{s^2+a^2}\)\(\sinh at\)\(\frac{a}{s^2-a^2}\)\(\cosh at\)\(\frac{s}{s^2-a^2}\)
Gamma function: \(\Gamma(n+1)=n!\), \(\Gamma(1/2)=\sqrt{\pi}\), \(\mathcal{L}\{t^n\}=\frac{\Gamma(n+1)}{s^{n+1}}\)
⚡Properties
Linearity
\(\mathcal{L}\{af+bg\}=aF+bG\)
First Shift
\(\mathcal{L}\{e^{at}f(t)\}=F(s-a)\)
Multiplication by \(t^n\)
\(\mathcal{L}\{t^nf(t)\}=(-1)^n\frac{d^nF}{ds^n}\)
Laplace of Derivatives
\(\mathcal{L}\{f'(t)\}=sF(s)-f(0)\)
\(\mathcal{L}\{f''(t)\}=s^2F(s)-sf(0)-f'(0)\)
\(\mathcal{L}\{f^{(n)}(t)\}=s^nF(s)-s^{n-1}f(0)-\cdots-f^{(n-1)}(0)\)
Laplace of Integral
\(\displaystyle\mathcal{L}\left\{\int_0^t f(u)du\right\}=\frac{F(s)}{s}\)
Periodic Function (period \(T\))
\(\displaystyle\mathcal{L}\{f(t)\}=\frac{\int_0^T e^{-st}f(t)dt}{1-e^{-sT}}\)
Unit Step & Second Shift
\(\mathcal{L}\{u(t-a)\}=e^{-as}/s\)
\(\mathcal{L}\{f(t-a)u(t-a)\}=e^{-as}F(s)\)
Unit Impulse (Dirac delta)
\(\mathcal{L}\{\delta(t-a)\}=e^{-as}\)
\(\displaystyle\int_0^\infty f(t)\delta(t-a)dt=f(a)\)
Convolution Theorem
\(\displaystyle (f*g)(t)=\int_0^t f(u)g(t-u)du\)
\(\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{F(s)G(s)\}=f(t)*g(t)\)
Inverse Laplace Examples
\(\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{\frac{1}{s-a}\}=e^{at}\)
\(\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{\frac{1}{(s-a)^2}\}=t e^{at}\)
\(\mathcal{L}^{-1}\{\frac{s}{s^2+a^2}\}=\cos at\)
ODE Solving Procedure
  1. Take Laplace of ODE using derivative properties
  2. Substitute initial conditions
  3. Solve for \(Y(s)=\mathcal{L}\{y(t)\}\)
  4. Find inverse Laplace (partial fractions / convolution)
Used for linear ODEs with constant coefficients; handles discontinuous forcing functions.
Unit 4 — Fourier Series & Transforms

∿Fourier Series — Full Range
Dirichlet's Conditions
  • Periodic & single-valued
  • Finite number of discontinuities per period
  • Finite number of maxima/minima per period
At a jump, series converges to \(\frac{f(x^-)+f(x^+)}{2}\).
Euler Formulae (period \(2\pi\))
\(\displaystyle f(x)=\frac{a_0}{2}+\sum_{n=1}^\infty (a_n\cos nx+b_n\sin nx)\)
\(a_0=\frac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}f(x)dx,\quad a_n=\frac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}f(x)\cos nx\,dx,\quad b_n=\frac{1}{\pi}\int_{-\pi}^{\pi}f(x)\sin nx\,dx\)
Arbitrary Period \(2l\)
\(\displaystyle f(x)=\frac{a_0}{2}+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\left[a_n\cos\frac{n\pi x}{l}+b_n\sin\frac{n\pi x}{l}\right]\)
\(a_0=\frac{1}{l}\int_{-l}^{l}f(x)dx,\quad a_n=\frac{1}{l}\int_{-l}^{l}f(x)\cos\frac{n\pi x}{l}dx,\quad b_n=\frac{1}{l}\int_{-l}^{l}f(x)\sin\frac{n\pi x}{l}dx\)
±Even & Odd Functions
Even — Cosine Series
\(\displaystyle f(x)=\frac{a_0}{2}+\sum a_n\cos\frac{n\pi x}{l},\quad a_n=\frac{2}{l}\int_0^l f(x)\cos\frac{n\pi x}{l}dx\)
Odd — Sine Series
\(\displaystyle f(x)=\sum b_n\sin\frac{n\pi x}{l},\quad b_n=\frac{2}{l}\int_0^l f(x)\sin\frac{n\pi x}{l}dx\)
½Half-Range Series
Half-Range Cosine (even extension)
\(a_0=\frac{2}{l}\int_0^l f(x)dx,\; a_n=\frac{2}{l}\int_0^l f(x)\cos\frac{n\pi x}{l}dx\)
Half-Range Sine (odd extension)
\(b_n=\frac{2}{l}\int_0^l f(x)\sin\frac{n\pi x}{l}dx\)
📐Parseval & Complex Form
Parseval's Identity
\(\displaystyle \frac{1}{l}\int_{-l}^{l}[f(x)]^2dx = \frac{a_0^2}{2}+\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}(a_n^2+b_n^2)\)
Complex Fourier Series (period \(2l\))
\(\displaystyle f(x)=\sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty}c_n e^{i n\pi x/l},\quad c_n=\frac{1}{2l}\int_{-l}^{l}f(x)e^{-i n\pi x/l}dx\)
Relation: \(c_0=a_0/2,\; c_n=(a_n-ib_n)/2,\; c_{-n}=(a_n+ib_n)/2\)
