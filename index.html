Integral Calculus Formula Handbook

Engineering Mathematics – Unit I

Double Integrals

$$\iint_R f(x,y)\,dA$$

General form of double integral over a region R in two variables.

$$\iint_R f(x,y)\,dx\,dy$$

Double integral evaluated by integrating with respect to y first and then x.

$$\iint_R f(x,y)\,dy\,dx$$

Double integral evaluated by integrating with respect to x first and then y.

Jacobian

$$ J=\frac{\partial(u,v)}{\partial(x,y)} =\begin{vmatrix} \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial u}{\partial y}\\ \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial v}{\partial y} \end{vmatrix} $$

Jacobian determinant used for transformation of variables in double integrals.

$$ J=\frac{\partial(u,v,w)}{\partial(x,y,z)} $$

Jacobian determinant used for transformation of variables in triple integrals.

Change of Variables

$$ \iint_R f(x,y)\,dxdy =\iint_{\bar R}\phi(u,v)\left|J\right|\,dudv $$

Transforms double integral into new variables using Jacobian determinant.

$$ \iiint_V f(x,y,z)\,dV =\iiint_{\bar V}\phi(u,v,w)\left|J\right|\,dudvdw $$

Transforms triple integral into new variables using Jacobian determinant.

Polar Coordinates

$$x=r\cos\theta,\qquad y=r\sin\theta$$

Conversion from Cartesian to polar coordinates.

$$dx\,dy=r\,dr\,d\theta$$

Area element transformation in polar coordinates.

$$ \iint_R f(x,y)\,dxdy =\iint_{\bar R} f(r\cos\theta,r\sin\theta)\,r\,dr\,d\theta $$

Double integral expressed in polar coordinate system.

Area & Volume

$$A=\iint_R dA$$

Area of region R using double integration.

$$V=\iint_R z\,dA$$

Volume under a surface over region R.

$$ f_{avg}=\frac{\iint_R f(x,y)\,dA}{\iint_R dA} $$

Average value of a function over a region.

Triple Integrals

$$\iiint_V f(x,y,z)\,dV$$

Triple integral over a 3D region V.

$$M=\iiint_V \rho\,dV$$

Mass of a solid with density function ρ.

Cylindrical Coordinates

$$ x=\rho\cos\phi,\quad y=\rho\sin\phi,\quad z=z $$

Conversion from Cartesian to cylindrical coordinates.

$$ dV=\rho\,d\rho\,d\phi\,dz $$

Volume element in cylindrical coordinates.

Spherical Coordinates

$$ x=r\sin\theta\cos\phi $$ $$ y=r\sin\theta\sin\phi $$ $$ z=r\cos\theta $$

Conversion from Cartesian to spherical coordinates.

$$ dV=r^2\sin\theta\,dr\,d\theta\,d\phi $$

Volume element in spherical coordinates.

Moment of Inertia (Plane Lamina)

$$I_x=\iint_D y^2\rho\,dA$$

Moment of inertia about x-axis for a lamina.

$$I_y=\iint_D x^2\rho\,dA$$

Moment of inertia about y-axis for a lamina.

$$I_O=I_x+I_y$$

Polar moment of inertia about origin.

Moment of Inertia (Solid)

$$I_x=\iiint_V (y^2+z^2)\rho\,dV$$

Moment of inertia about x-axis for a solid body.

$$I_y=\iiint_V (x^2+z^2)\rho\,dV$$

Moment of inertia about y-axis for a solid body.

$$I_z=\iiint_V (x^2+y^2)\rho\,dV$$

Moment of inertia about z-axis for a solid body.

Centre of Gravity / Centre of Mass

$$ \bar x=\frac{\iint_D x\rho\,dA}{M}, \qquad \bar y=\frac{\iint_D y\rho\,dA}{M} $$

Coordinates of center of mass for a lamina.

$$ \bar x=\frac{\iiint_V x\rho\,dV}{M}, \quad \bar y=\frac{\iiint_V y\rho\,dV}{M}, \quad \bar z=\frac{\iiint_V z\rho\,dV}{M} $$

Coordinates of center of mass for a 3D solid body.
