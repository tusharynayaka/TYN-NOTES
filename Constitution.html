Constitution of India - Articles, Parts & Acts Reference

Fundamental Rights & Citizenship

  • Article 5 (1950) – Citizenship at the commencement of the Constitution.
  • Article 6 (1950) – Citizenship rights of persons who migrated from Pakistan to India.
  • Article 7 (1950) – Rights of migrants who moved to Pakistan and later returned to India.
  • Article 8 (1950) – Citizenship of Indians residing outside India.
  • Article 9 (1950) – Voluntary acquisition of foreign citizenship results in loss of Indian citizenship.
  • Article 12 (1950) – Defines "State" for enforcement of Fundamental Rights.
  • Article 13(1) (1950) – Pre-Constitution laws inconsistent with Fundamental Rights become void.
  • Article 13(2) (1950) – State cannot make laws violating Fundamental Rights.
  • Article 14 (1950) – Equality before law and equal protection of laws.
  • Article 17 (1950) – Abolition of untouchability and its practice.
  • Article 19(1)(a) (1950) – Freedom of speech and expression.
  • Article 19(1)(b) (1950) – Freedom to assemble peacefully without arms.
  • Article 19(1)(c) (1950) – Freedom to form associations or unions.
  • Article 19(1)(d) (1950) – Freedom to move freely throughout India.
  • Article 19(1)(e) (1950) – Freedom to reside and settle anywhere in India.
  • Article 20 (1950) – Protection against ex-post facto laws, double jeopardy and self-incrimination.
  • Article 21 (1950) – Protection of life and personal liberty.
  • Article 25 (1950) – Freedom of conscience and religion.
  • Article 26 (1950) – Freedom to manage religious affairs.
  • Article 29 (1950) – Protection of language, culture and educational rights.
  • Article 30 (1950) – Minority rights to establish and administer educational institutions.
  • Article 32 (1950) – Right to constitutional remedies through Supreme Court.
  • Article 33 (1950) – Parliament may restrict Fundamental Rights of armed forces and security personnel.

Constitutional Amendment Acts

  • 24th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1971 – Restored Parliament's power to amend Fundamental Rights.
  • 25th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1971 – Curtailed Right to Property and strengthened DPSPs.
  • 40th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976 – Expanded Parliament's authority over certain matters and Ninth Schedule entries.
  • 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1976 – Known as the "Mini Constitution"; added Fundamental Duties and strengthened Centre.
  • 44th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1978 – Restored democratic safeguards after Emergency; removed Right to Property as Fundamental Right.
  • 48th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1984 – Extended President's Rule in Punjab.
  • 59th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1988 – Emergency-related provisions concerning Punjab.
  • 60th Constitutional Amendment Act, 1988 – Increased ceiling on profession tax.
  • 61st Constitutional Amendment Act, 1988 – Reduced voting age from 21 years to 18 years.
  • 62nd Constitutional Amendment Act, 1989 – Extended reservation of seats for SCs, STs and Anglo-Indians.

Central Acts Mentioned

  • Information Technology Act, 2000 – Legal recognition of electronic records, digital signatures and cybercrime regulation.
  • Indian Contract Act, 1872 – Governs contracts and agreements in India.
  • Specific Relief Act, 1963 – Provides remedies such as specific performance and injunctions.
  • Code of Civil Procedure (CPC), 1908 – Procedure for civil court proceedings.
  • Transfer of Property Act, 1882 – Governs transfer of immovable property.
  • Child Labour (Prohibition and Regulation) Act, 1986 – Regulates and prohibits child labour in specified occupations.
  • Government of India Act, 1935 – Constitutional framework before Independence; basis for many constitutional provisions.

Information Technology Act Sections

  • Section 10A (2008) – Legal validity of electronic contracts.
  • Section 66A (2008, struck down in 2015) – Punishment for offensive online messages; declared unconstitutional in Shreya Singhal case.
  • Section 66B (2008) – Receiving stolen computer resources or communication devices.
  • Section 66D (2008) – Cheating by personation using computer resources.
  • Section 66F (2008) – Cyber terrorism.
  • Section 2(ta) (2008) – Definition of Electronic Signature.
